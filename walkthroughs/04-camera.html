04 — The Eyes of the Player

An orbit camera that circles the player using three values: alpha, beta, and radius.

What this file does

Creates an ArcRotateCamera — Babylon's orbit camera. It always points at a target (the player) and rotates around it like a satellite.

Alpha is the horizontal angle — how far left or right the camera has rotated around the player.

Beta is the vertical angle — how high or low the camera sits (0 = directly above, π/2 = eye level). Capped at π/2.5 so it can't go underground.

Radius is how far the camera is from the player. Clamped between 5 (close) and 40 (far). Mouse scroll controls this.

Every frame, the camera's target is updated to follow the player's current position so it always stays locked on.

Interactive — Drag the Sliders

Move the sliders to see how alpha, beta, and radius change the camera position around the player.
